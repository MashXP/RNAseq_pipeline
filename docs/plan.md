# Research Plan: Kromastat (Krom) vs. Romidepsin (Romi)

## 1. Project Purpose & Context
- **Kromastat (Krom)**: New drug candidate in development.
- **Romidepsin (Romi)**: Established standard-of-care drug already on the market.
- **Goal**: To continue development of Krom, we must demonstrate it is **superior** in efficacy (killing cancer) and/or **inferior** in toxicity (safer for healthy cells) compared to Romi.

## 2. Experimental Strategy
- **Prerequisite**: Before human clinical trials, we test on dogs.
- **In Vitro Validation**: Testing on both Human and Dog cell lines simultaneously to ensure cross-species consistency.
- **Next Steps**: If in vitro results are positive and consistent, we move to clinical trials in dogs, followed by human clinical trials.

## 3. Core Biological Questions
For each species (Human/Dog) and cell line, we ask: **Is Krom better than Romi?**

### Cell Line Categories:
1. **Healthy/Control Lines ("Lành")**:
   - **Human**: H9
   - **Dog**: UL1
   - *Goal*: Show Krom is less toxic or has a cleaner profile in these cells.
2. **Aggressive/Malignant Lines ("Ác dữ")**:
   - **Human**: SUPM2
   - **Dog**: CNK98
   - *Goal*: Show Krom has a stronger anti-cancer transcriptomic signature than Romi in these cells.

## 4. Analytical Success Criteria
1. **Direct Contrast**: Direct comparison of `Krom_vs_Romi` within each cell line.
2. **Potency Check**: Are more "cell death" or "tumor suppressor" pathways activated by Krom in SUPM2/CNK98?
3. **Safety Check**: Are fewer "stress" or "toxicity" pathways activated by Krom in H9/UL1?
4. **Conservation**: Do the same "superior" markers translate from Dog (CNK98) to Human (SUPM2)?
